# Palms atlas — research findings & proposed shape

_Synthesis of the source material you provided (Scott Zona bio; six issues of PALMS, the IPS journal,
v69 n1–n4 2025 and v70 n1 2026; nine IPS monthly newsletters, Jun 2025 – Jun 2026) plus a survey of the
public palm-data landscape. This is the evidence base for the project spec. Written to mirror the Quercus
project's structure, since we're using that as the scaffold._

_Note on scope: the family is **Arecaceae** (Palmae) — the palms. (Not Araceae, the aroids.) Everything
below is palms._

---

## 1. Who the audience actually is — two wings of one community

The material shows a single society (**the International Palm Society**, founded 1955–56, turning 70 in
2026) that contains two distinct but overlapping audiences. The Quercus project had an expert/newcomer
tension; the palm community's split is sharper and both wings are large and organized.

**The palm scientists** (voice of the PALMS journal; editors William Baker & Scott Zona at Kew, John
Dransfield associate editor). What they do, issue after issue:
- **Discover, name, and delimit** rare palms. Every issue has a new-species description, a first-country
  record, or a "not-extinct-after-all" rediscovery. The emotional register is the hunt.
- **Argue where the species line falls** — *Sabal tamaulipensis* vs *S. minor*, *Phoenix atlantica* vs the
  date palm, the sinking of *Wallichia* into *Arenga*, the *Dypsis*/*Chrysalidocarpus* split. Delimitation
  is a genuine obsession, settled with a mix of morphology, hidden anatomical characters, and molecular
  trees.
- **Resolve questions with nuclear-gene phylogenomics** (target capture, 150–3,400 genes) — the
  Kew / Gothenburg / Aarhus / Montgomery Botanical Center cluster. They already build "workbench" figures:
  a cladogram with support values beside morphology and a map.
- **Tell it as field expedition** — first-person, day-by-day, machetes and dugout canoes and named local
  guides. The journey is the article.

**The palm growers / enthusiasts** (voice of the IPS newsletter; editor Jason Dewees, a San Francisco
horticulturist — note the handle "loulufan," loulu being the Hawaiian *Pritchardia*). What they obsess
over:
- **Growing palms where they "shouldn't" grow.** This is the single strongest signal in the newsletters —
  zone-pushing, microclimate engineering, cold-damage survivor stories, the celebrated few hardy species.
  They explicitly track the USDA hardiness-zone shift (2012 → 2023) as lived experience.
- **Field identification, phone-first** — standing in cloud forest arguing "is this one acaulescent?" and
  refreshing monograph PDFs on their phones.
- **Sourcing rare seed** (and getting it through customs), then **sharing seedlings** — auctions and
  inter-garden donations are the distribution network.
- **Private-garden culture** — open-house tours of members' collections are the core social ritual; trophy
  specimens confer status.
- **Biennial expeditions** to "palmy places" (Vietnam 2026, Fiji 2028) — pilgrimages that sell out.

**What both wings share, and what the project must center:**
- **Conservation.** The society motto is literally "Conserve. Learn. Grow." Extinction risk is woven
  through the journal and drives the newsletter's flagship fundraising (Save the Species; the *Sabinaria*
  and *Tahina* reserve campaigns). Over half the family may be threatened (see §3).
- **A collector-conservationist double identity.** The same rare palm is "highly sought after by
  enthusiasts" and "Critically Endangered" in the same paragraph. Seeds distributed to growers become the
  ex-situ safety net (the *Syagrus santosii* and *Sabinaria*-flowering-in-Hawaii stories).
- **Island endemism and micro-endemism** — Madagascar, Hawaii, Fiji, New Caledonia, Philippine karst,
  Pacific atolls. Palms restricted to one river, one limestone peak, one lagoon islet.
- **Ethnobotany** — rattan cane and weaving, thatch, palm heart, sago, date, coconut, betel, palm wine,
  carnauba wax. Much larger than the human-use dimension was for oaks, and both wings love it.

## 2. What the palms *are* — the phenomena worth building around

Recurring "wait, palms do that?" material from the journals, the raw fuel for reveals and expeditions:

- **Palms are frost-intolerant, and their distribution draws a thermal line on the planet.** This is the
  deep organizing fact of palm biogeography (and the growers' whole world): with a handful of hardy
  exceptions, palms cannot take sustained freezing, so where palms grow *is* a map of where it doesn't hard
  freeze. Fossil palms are used as a paleoclimate thermometer for exactly this reason.
- **Island adaptive radiations that blur into hybridization.** Hawaiian *Pritchardia* (~24 species, the
  only palm genus native to Hawaii, radiated across the volcanic chronosequence) — and the closer you look
  the less discrete the species become (ongoing gene flow and admixture; fresh 2026 data in v70 n1).
  Madagascar's *Dypsis* (largest palm radiation) and *Ravenea* hotspot.
- **Extreme micro-endemism you can watch collapse.** *Ravenea* — 10 of ~25 species crammed into one
  county-sized corner of SE Madagascar, several down to a single locality, most Critically Endangered.
- **Habitat specialists in impossible places.** *Ravenea musicalis*, the palm that germinates and grows
  **submerged in flowing rivers**; *Nypa fruticans*, the only mangrove palm; *Ceroxylon*, wax palms to
  60 m in the Andes at 3,000 m; karst-peak endemics.
- **The wild "blue-water coconut"** (*Cocos nucifera* var. *palmyrensis*) — a never-domesticated coconut
  from one atoll, its giant thick-husked triangular fruit built for ocean voyaging, vs. the round
  domesticated grocery coconut.
- **Sister genera split across the Neotropics** — *Sabinaria* (Darién Gap, described 2013, its own genus)
  and *Itaya* (Peruvian Amazon) are closest relatives on opposite sides of northern South America.
- **The rattans** — climbing palms (*Calamus*, the largest palm genus, ~500 species), with grapnel whips,
  ant-house symbioses (*Korthalsia* ants drum in unison when disturbed), and the cane economy.
- **Spectacular life histories** — hapaxanthy (*Corypha*, *Metroxylon* sago, *Caryota* fishtails: grow for
  decades, flower once in a giant terminal burst, die).
- **Evolutionary anachronism** — Hawaiian *Pritchardia* fruits were dispersed uphill by giant flightless
  birds now extinct; without them fruit only rolls downhill, trapping palms at low elevations where pigs,
  rats, and the coconut rhinoceros beetle concentrate.

## 3. The public data landscape — unusually favorable

Palms are, if anything, **better served by open data than oaks were**, because a near-complete family
scaffold ships as a handful of cleanly-licensed files. No interactive tool currently integrates any of it
(see prior art below), so the integration layer is open white space.

### The ingestable core (open, redistributable)
- **WCVP (World Checklist of Vascular Plants, Kew)** — the name authority and join spine, exactly as in the
  oak project. ~2,600 accepted species, ~181 genera, 5 subfamilies (Calamoideae, Nypoideae, Coryphoideae,
  Ceroxyloideae, Arecoideae). Native/introduced ranges coded to **TDWG WGSRPD level-3 botanical
  countries**. Bulk Darwin Core Archive from Kew SFTP, mirrored on GBIF. **CC-BY 4.0**, versioned DOIs.
  Descends from Govaerts & Dransfield's *World Checklist of Palms*.
- **PalmTraits 1.0** (Kissling et al. 2019, *Scientific Data*; Dryad doi:10.5061/dryad.ts45225) — **the
  crown jewel, CC0.** Traits for **2,557 species** (effectively the whole family): growth form
  (climbing / erect / acaulescent, solitary vs clustered, understory vs canopy), max stem height &
  diameter, armature, **leaf type (pinnate / palmate / costapalmate / bipinnate)**, blade/rachis/petiole
  lengths, **fruit size, shape, and color**. The same download **also bundles the complete species-level
  phylogeny (`TREE.nex`, Faurby 2016) and a species × botanical-country range matrix + TDWG level-3
  shapefile.** One CC0 file ≈ traits + tree + ranges for the whole family.
- **Faurby, Eiserhardt, Baker & Svenning 2016** — the only **complete species-level palm phylogeny**
  (~2,539 tips; data-poor species placed by taxonomic constraint, so terminal placements are uncertain and
  should be shown as such). CC0. Deep-node dating and a rigorous nuclear backbone come from **Kew PAFTOL /
  Zuntini & Baker 2024** (CC-BY).
- **GBIF occurrences** — family taxonKey **7681**; ~1.14M georeferenced records (~86% of 1.33M total).
  License-filterable (~78% CC0/CC-BY); DOI-minted downloads. Same per-point provenance model as oaks.
- **Bellot et al. 2022** (*Nature Ecology & Evolution*, doi:10.1038/s41559-022-01858-0) — **the conservation
  layer that matters.** Machine-learning extinction-risk predictions for ~1,381 unassessed palms, which
  combined with the ~508 IUCN-assessed give a status for **~75% of the family — and predict over half of
  all palms are threatened.** This fills the gap left by IUCN's restrictive license. _[Open item: confirm
  the supplement's reuse license before rehosting the rows vs. linking out.]_
- **iNaturalist CC photos** — ~149k CC0/CC-BY/CC-BY-NC palm records via the AWS Open Data mirror; per-image
  license, filterable. Same photo pipeline as oaks.
- **Palm genomes** — oil palm (*Elaeis*), date (*Phoenix dactylifera*), coconut (*Cocos*), betel (*Areca*),
  rattan (*Calamus*) — all public on NCBI/INSDC. (Notably, **no palm is in Ensembl Plants** — a genome
  layer would itself be white space.)

### Restricted — link out, do not rehost
- **IUCN Red List** — authoritative but not open; redistribution barred without a waiver. Only ~20% of the
  family is assessed. Use the v4 API for **display-only** current status with attribution; lead the
  conservation story with the openly-licensable Bellot predictions instead.
- **Map of Life** palm ranges — viewable, inherits IUCN terms; use PalmTraits/WCVP ranges instead.
- **The hobbyist gold mine — Palmpedia, PACSOA wiki, PalmTalk forum, Rare Palm Seeds, Trebrown, Zone
  Denial, RHS** — the richest cultivation and cold-hardiness content anywhere, but all-rights-reserved /
  no license / bot-blocked. Facts (a hardiness temperature) aren't copyrightable but their curated
  expression is; re-derive hardiness from primary observations (GBIF/iNat occurrences vs. climate, the
  CC-BY-SA Wikipedia "hardy palms" list, USDA public-domain zone geography) rather than lifting text.

### Prior art / white space
POWO shows static per-species range choropleths with no tree; Palmweb is aging legacy infrastructure;
PalmTraits has no front-end; the Faurby/PAFTOL trees are files, not apps. **No tool links a palm phylogeny
to a world map to traits with brushed selection.** That linked integration is the ownable product — the
same white space the oak project found, but with better raw data and an even larger, more organized
audience waiting for it.

## 4. Proposed shape (draft — the load-bearing choices are still open)

Poured into the Quercus architecture: **two co-equal anchors (phylogeny + world map), analytical lenses
dropped onto them, a signature reveal, authored expeditions, conservation as a cross-cut, editorial-science
aesthetic.** Adapted to palm content:

**The spine.** The complete Faurby species-level tree (all ~2,600 species, terminal uncertainty shown) +
a world map. Because PalmTraits/Faurby/WCVP give the whole family's tree + traits + ranges in one open
download, the palm project can plausibly go **family-complete from day one** for tree+traits+ranges —
unlike the oak project, which started slice-only because a genus-wide tree and traits weren't available.
(Occurrence points and the flagship reveal may still start on a tractable slice.)

**Candidate lenses (drawn from what these people care about):**
- **Climate & the palm line** — frost intolerance, hardiness, palms as a living thermometer and
  paleoclimate proxy. Serves both the growers' hardiness obsession and the scientists' biogeography.
- **Radiation & endemism** — island adaptive radiations, micro-endemism hotspots, and where species
  boundaries blur into hybridization (Hawaii, Madagascar, Fiji, New Caledonia).
- **Form & function** — the PalmTraits disparity: climbing rattans vs. canopy tree palms vs. stemless
  understory palms; leaf architecture; fruit size and dispersal. Palms have spectacular morphological range.
- **People & palms** — ethnobotany and economic botany (rattan, date, coconut, oil palm, sago, thatch,
  palm heart, betel, wax). Distinctly palm; both wings love it.
- **Conservation cross-cut** — Bellot ML risk (open, ~75% coverage), the ex-situ safety-net story, harvest
  pressure on rattans, the collector-as-conservationist loop. First-class dimension, everywhere.

**A grower "field companion" surface** — the newsletters explicitly ask for this: a "will this palm survive
my winter?" hardiness matcher (drop a pin / enter a zone → what grows outdoors, marginally, or only under
glass), phone-first field ID, a sourcing/availability layer, and a garden/feral-occurrence log tied to
iNaturalist. Open question below is how central this is.

**Flagship reveal (money-shot) candidates** — the single load-bearing decision, put to you in the message
accompanying this doc. Leading options: (A) the palm line / hardiness / paleoclimate thermometer;
(B) an island radiation that blurs into hybridization up close; (C) extinction-risk-led "half the palms
could vanish."

## 5. Key differences from the Quercus build (so we don't blindly copy)
1. **A family, not a genus** — ~2,600 species across 181 genera and 5 subfamilies, vastly more
   morphologically and geographically disparate than one genus of oaks. Naming/title needs a decision
   (there's no single Latin genus to name it after).
2. **Data completeness arrives on day one** — PalmTraits + Faurby + WCVP make a family-complete
   tree+traits+ranges scaffold available immediately, so the "start with a 5-species slice, scale later"
   arc may not apply the way it did for oaks.
3. **A much larger, more organized enthusiast wing** — the grower/hardiness audience may deserve co-equal
   billing rather than the secondary "mobile field companion" treatment oaks gave it.
4. **Conservation status must lead with predictions, not IUCN** — IUCN is license-restricted and covers
   only ~20% of palms; the open Bellot ML layer covers ~75% and is the stronger, shippable spine.
5. **A distinct signature phenomenon** — the oak reveal was about species boundaries (chloroplast capture);
   the palm project should have its own thesis, most likely the frost line / climate story, which is
   palm-defining and unifies both audiences.

## 6. Decisions (locked 2026-07-07)
- **The money-shot: the palm line** — palms as a living thermometer (frost intolerance draws a thermal
  line on the planet, the hardy renegades that cheat it, plus deep-time and future-warming axes). Serves
  both audiences; palm-defining; distinct from the oak thesis.
- **Audience balance: the grower "will it grow where I live?" surface is co-equal** with the expert
  workbench — not the secondary treatment oaks gave their field companion.
- **Scope: family-complete from day one** (~2,600 species; tree + traits + TDWG ranges from the open
  download); the rich-slice treatment is reserved for occurrence points and the flagship reveal.
- **Conservation posture: lead with the open Bellot 2022 ML risk predictions** (~75% coverage), with IUCN
  category as a labeled display-only overlay for the ~20% formally assessed.

Still open: **name/title** (the family has no single genus to borrow — working title **Palmae**), and
confirming the **build path** (production build reusing the Quercus design system + code, vs. a fresh
design-prototype pass — assumed the former, since we're scaffolding on Quercus's finished design).

See `palms-spec.md` for the full spec built on these decisions.
